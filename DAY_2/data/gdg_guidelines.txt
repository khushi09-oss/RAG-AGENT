GOOGLE DEVELOPER GROUPS (GDG)
Community Guidelines & Member Handbook
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

ABOUT GDG

Google Developer Groups (GDG) are community groups for college and university 
students interested in Google developer technologies. Students from all 
undergraduate or graduate programs with an interest in growing as a developer 
are welcome.

By joining a GDG, students grow their knowledge in a peer-to-peer learning 
environment and build solutions for local businesses and their community.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────
MEMBERSHIP

Who Can Join:
- All students enrolled in undergraduate or graduate programs
- Alumni within 1 year of graduation
- Anyone passionate about technology and learning

How to Join:
1. Visit gdg.community.dev
2. Find your local GDG chapter
3. Click "Join this group"
4. Create or sign in with your Google account
5. Complete your profile
6. Start participating in events

Membership Benefits:
- Free workshops and training sessions
- Access to Google Cloud credits
- Networking with industry professionals
- Certificates for completed programs
- Career guidance and mentorship

───────────────────────────────────────────────────
EVENTS & ACTIVITIES

Types of Events:

Workshops
- Hands-on technical training
- Duration: 2-4 hours
- Topics: AI/ML, Cloud, Mobile, Web Development
- Materials provided
- Certificates upon completion

Study Jams
- Self-paced learning with group support
- Meet weekly for 4-8 weeks
- Follow Google training programs

Hackathons
- 24-48 hour coding competitions
- Team-based problem solving
- Prizes and recognition

Tech Talks
- 60-minute presentations
- Industry speakers
- Latest technology trends

Event Schedule:
- Workshops: Every Saturday, 9:00 AM - 12:30 PM
- Study Jams: Tuesdays & Thursdays, 6:00 PM - 8:00 PM
- Tech Talks: First Friday of each month, 7:00 PM
- Hackathons: Quarterly
- DevFest: Annual, typically in October

Event Requirements:
- Laptop with charger
- Required software pre-installed
- Valid student ID for first-time attendees

Venue Facilities:
- High-speed WiFi
- Power outlets at all seats
- Coffee and refreshments

───────────────────────────────────────────────────
CODE OF CONDUCT

Our Pledge:
We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all.

Expected Behavior:
- Be respectful and inclusive
- Listen actively and speak constructively
- Welcome newcomers warmly
- Give credit to others' ideas

Unacceptable Behavior:
- Harassment of any kind
- Discriminatory language
- Personal attacks

Reporting:
Contact organizers immediately or email conduct@gdg.org
